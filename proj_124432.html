The field of personalized medicine has gained significant momentum in recent years, driven by the growing understanding of the unique genetic, molecular, and environmental factors that influence an individual's susceptibility to disease, response to treatment, and disease progression. Personalized medicine aims to tailor healthcare interventions to the specific needs and characteristics of each patient, moving away from the one-size-fits-all approach that has traditionally dominated the healthcare landscape.

The rapid advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) have presented significant opportunities for the field of personalized medicine. AI techniques, such as machine learning and deep learning, have shown great potential in accelerating the development and implementation of personalized healthcare strategies. This paper provides an overview of the key applications of AI in personalized medicine. It explores how AI can be leveraged to enhance various aspects of the personalized medicine ecosystem, including disease diagnosis, drug discovery and repurposing, treatment selection, and patient monitoring and management. The review discusses how AI-powered predictive models can analyze complex biomedical data, including genomics, imaging, and electronic health records, to enable more accurate disease risk assessment and early intervention. Additionally, it examines how AI can facilitate the identification of novel drug targets and the repurposing of existing drugs for personalized therapies. Furthermore, the paper highlights the role of AI in optimizing treatment selection by providing decision support systems that integrate patient-specific data with evidence-based guidelines. It also explores the use of AI-powered wearable devices and remote monitoring technologies to enable continuous patient monitoring and personalized care delivery. The paper concludes by discussing the challenges and future directions in the integration of AI into personalized medicine, including the need for robust data infrastructure, ethical considerations, and regulatory frameworks. Overall, this review underscores the transformative potential of AI in advancing the field of personalized medicine and improving patient outcomes.
